My name is Xiuhtezcatl Martinez- and this is my story. My first name comes from the Aztec language. It’s pronounced ‘Shoe-Tez- Caht’. From a young age I began to find books in school and do research about the effects of our everyday on the climate. When I was six I started to warn people of the dangers of climate change. I began a campaign to remove pesticides from local parks. I was inspired by being able to make a difference in my community. I decided to tackle more issues in my community. I campaigned to stop fracking in my state and then joined with a group of young activists to sue the government for failing to protect the environment for future generations. My story has seen me move from being a local activist to a regional activist. I try to teach young people about the environment and why it is worth fighting to protect.
